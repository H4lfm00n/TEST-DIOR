The Car

What is a Supra?

The Toyota Supra is a high-performance sports car produced by Toyota since 1978. Evolved from the Celica platform, the Supra grew into its own identity — rear-wheel drive, inline-6 power, and a chassis tuned for driving at the limit.

Its name comes from the Latin prefix meaning "above" or "beyond." That name stuck — the Supra became a benchmark for Japanese performance, celebrated globally in motorsport, tuner culture, and pop culture alike.

History

Generations

A40 / A50

1978 – 1981

The original Supra, derived from the Celica. A long hood, inline-6 engine, and sporting ambition set the tone for everything that followed.

A60

1981 – 1986

Grew bigger and more powerful. Introduced fuel injection and became a grand tourer as much as a sports car — fast and comfortable over long distances.

A70

1986 – 1993

The Supra went independent of the Celica. Turbocharged variants pushed well over 230 hp. Boxy, muscular styling made it an icon of its era.

A80

1993 – 2002

The legend. The 2JZ-GTE engine became the most famous inline-6 in tuner history. Stock at 320 hp, endlessly tuneable to 1000+ hp.

GR Supra (A90)

2019 – present

Reborn after a 17-year hiatus, co-developed with BMW. The B58 inline-6 delivers 382–503 hp. Controversial in origin, respected in execution.

Engineering

The 2JZ Engine

The 2JZ-GTE is why the A80 Supra became a cultural touchstone. Toyota's 3.0L twin-turbocharged inline-6 was overbuilt from the factory — thick cast-iron block, forged internals, and tolerances that could handle massive power upgrades without internal modifications.

Tuners quickly discovered they could double or triple the factory output. 600, 800, 1000+ horsepower on the stock block became routine.

Impact

Legacy

The Supra transcends being just a car. It appeared in the original Fast & Furious film in 2001, exposing it to a generation who hadn't grown up with it. It dominates top-time attack leaderboards. It inspired a genre of aftermarket parts and tuning culture that still thrives today.

Even two decades after production ended, clean A80 Supras command six-figure prices at auction. Few Japanese cars have earned that kind of staying power.
